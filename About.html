Grant Goals

The overall goal for this project is to develop an HSI Student Success Servingness Framework that focuses on pedagogy development through evidence-based teaching approaches, combined with enhanced student peer mentoring and professional development of graduate students and faculty, with the aim of improving student learning, student sense of belongingness and overall success.  



HSI Framework

Development of an HSI Student Servingness Framework

Course Improvement and Professional Development

Pedagogical development of engineering courses focused on Computational Literacy, which includes professional development of graduate students and faculty

Policy Reviews

Review current policies, practices, and structures for undergraduate students with a focus on diversity, equity, and inclusion

Enhance Education

Development and subsequent expansion of Peer Mentoring Program and TA Teaching Academy

As we planned for the Track 3 Project, we reflected on the Track 2 Grant Project. Reflecting on Track 2, we plan to build our knowledge and incorporate new ideas and expertise to create a framework that supports institution-wide structural and systemic changes to enhance undergraduate STEM education for Latinx students. The HSI Student Servingness Framework will allow for institutional change through curriculum changes, policy review, and expanding peer mentoring and other activities. The expected outcomes from Track 3 are:

    Increase the retention, graduation and persistence rates of the students

    Improve the students self-efficacy and sense of belongingness

    Give faculty opportunities to develop innovative, equity-centered evidence-based best practice teaching approaches that enhance student learning and equity in the classroom

    Provide students with marketable skills to be successful in the professional world

Objective 1

To accomplish our first objective, we have acknowledged the theoretical foundations of what constitutes HSI Servingness. The theory of HSI Servingness begins with the notion that Hispanic-Serving institutions are federally classified Institutions that meet the full-time undergraduate threshold of 25% Latinx and 50% Pell Grant eligibility. It is argued that HSIs are in a position to close the nation's aracial degree attainment gaps, particularly in STEM Fields. However, this project proposes an Institutional shift away from enrollment, focusing on organizational commitment, intentionality, and broader impacts for Latinx students. However, while still acknowledging Latinx enrollment, the remaining question is how to best serve Latinx students from an institutional transformation perspective that aligns with the main concerns of HSI servingness theory. Enrollment alone does not demonstrate student servingness amongst Latinx students, so how do we demonstrate how well Latinx students are being served at an HSI?

Research has been conducted on conceptualizing “servingness” for Latinx students at HSIs in a deconstructed manner to recognize that how well Latinx students are served by an HSI is more fully understood when viewed through multiple indicators of service, primarily associated with academic outcomes, experiences, organizational dimensions, and external influences (Garcia, Núñez, & Sansone, 2019). HSI servingness theory has suggested that servingness should also involve how the institution structures its level of service for Latinx students (Gonzales, 2015).

Specifically, this means that HSI 4 servingness involves:

- Policies that support Latinx students
- Programs that offer comprehensive services to Latinx students
- Practices that assist Latinx students and connect them to resources through intentional planning and investment by the organization

This approach can enhance the success, experiences, and opportunities of Latinx students and reduce racial inequality in educational outcomes.
